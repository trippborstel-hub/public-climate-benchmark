ESRS E1-1 · CDP C3
Transition Plan for Climate Change Mitigation
Partial

Kellanova's climate transition plan is anchored in the Better Days Promise — a commitment to create better days for 4 billion people by 2030. Following the October 2023 spin-off from Kellogg Company, Kellanova inherited and reaffirmed the company's existing climate commitments, including a net-zero-by-2050 target approved by SBTi.

The company committed to the SBTi Net-Zero Standard in November 2023, establishing aggressive near-term targets aligned with a 1.5°C pathway. However, as of the Mars acquisition closing in December 2025, Kellanova had not yet received full SBTi validation for its targets — the commitment was made but the validation process was still underway.

Kellanova's transition plan is structured around three pillars: operational energy transformation (100% renewable electricity by 2030), supply chain decarbonization (focusing on agriculture, packaging, and logistics), and deforestation elimination across primary commodities.

Analyst Assessment

Kellanova's transition plan is credible in structure but faces a unique challenge: the company existed as an independent entity for only ~2 years (Oct 2023 – Dec 2025) before being acquired by Mars. During that window, it reaffirmed inherited Kellogg commitments and committed to SBTi Net-Zero, but had limited time to establish an independent track record. Going forward, Kellanova's targets will be integrated into Mars' broader net-zero framework, which is SBTi-validated and backed by $2.7B in climate investment.

GHG Emissions Profile
Partial

Kellanova's emissions profile inherits from Kellogg Company's pre-spin-off data. The company measures GHG emissions across Scope 1, 2, and 3 according to the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard, with independent verification by Apex Companies LLC. As of the 2022 reporting year (the last full-year data under the Kellogg umbrella), the combined entity had achieved a 31.1% absolute reduction in Scope 1 & 2 emissions versus the 2015 baseline.

Post-spin-off emissions data for Kellanova as a standalone entity has limited public availability. The company committed to publishing its first independent Social and Environmental Report (detailing 2024 progress) in 2025, but the Mars acquisition in December 2025 complicated this timeline.

Kellanova's energy strategy is diversified across geographies. The company uses solar energy at manufacturing facilities in India, South Africa, Thailand, and Malaysia; wind energy via a long-term virtual power purchase agreement (VPPA) in North America; and hydropower providing 100% of electricity at operations in Colombia. The 100% renewable electricity target was accelerated from the original Kellogg Company deadline of 2050 to 2030.

The RE100 membership and multi-technology approach across geographies positions Kellanova well for continued progress. Under Mars ownership, Kellanova's energy transition will benefit from Mars' larger-scale procurement power and existing 59% renewable electricity achievement.

Climate Risk Identification & Resilience
Partial

Kellanova is a TCFD signatory and has conducted climate scenario analysis to assess physical and transition risks across its value chain. As a global snacking company reliant on agricultural commodities — including wheat, corn, palm oil, cocoa, soy, and rice — Kellanova faces significant exposure to climate-driven disruptions in crop yields and supply chain logistics.

The 2023 10-K filing identifies climate change as a material business risk, citing potential impacts on raw material costs, agricultural productivity, and regulatory compliance.

Gap Assessment

Kellanova's TCFD disclosures cover risk identification but lack quantified financial impact estimates. Scenario analysis methodology (specific scenarios, temperature pathways, time horizons) is not fully disclosed. Under ESRS E1-2 and E1-3, more granular physical and transition risk quantification would be required. The Mars acquisition may improve disclosure quality, as Mars has more mature risk assessment frameworks — though Mars also has gaps in financial quantification.

Carbon Credits, Offsets & Removals
Not Disclosed

Kellanova has not publicly disclosed any use of carbon credits, offsets, or removal strategies. The company's SBTi-committed targets are gross absolute targets with no indication of credit netting — consistent with SBTi requirements for near-term targets.

No internal carbon pricing mechanism has been disclosed. The company's climate strategy is focused on direct operational reductions (renewable energy, efficiency) and supply chain engagement (deforestation, regenerative agriculture) rather than offset-based approaches.

ESRS E1-9 · E1-11 · CDP C3.4
Anticipated Financial Effects
Not Disclosed

Kellanova's financial disclosures related to climate are limited. The company acknowledged climate as a material risk in its 10-K filings but did not provide quantified financial impact estimates for physical or transition risks. Climate-related CapEx, OpEx, and revenue-at-risk figures were not separately disclosed.

The $36 billion Mars acquisition fundamentally changes the financial context. Kellanova's climate investments and costs will now be subsumed within Mars' $2.7B climate CapEx program and broader Sustainability Investment Fund.

CSRD Readiness Gap

Kellanova's financial climate disclosures represent the most significant gap relative to ESRS requirements. ESRS E1-9 and E1-11 require quantified financial effects from physical and transition risks, reconciled to financial statements. As a now-private entity under Mars, Kellanova's future CSRD obligations will depend on Mars' European reporting scope and whether Kellanova's operations fall within the consolidation boundary.

Governance & Accountability
Partial

Kellanova maintained a Board-level Social Responsibility and Public Policy Committee with oversight of environmental and sustainability matters. CEO Steve Cahillane served as Chairman, President, and CEO through the Mars acquisition. The company had a dedicated VP of Sustainability role and a sustainability team responsible for executing climate commitments.

CDP reporting has been maintained annually since 2016. Post-acquisition, Kellanova's governance structure will be absorbed into Mars' framework, which includes a dedicated Chief Climate Officer and board-level sustainability committee.
