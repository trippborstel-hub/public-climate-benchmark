Transition Plan for Climate Change Mitigation
Partial

Hain Celestial's climate transition plan operates under the company's Global Impact Strategy, structured around three pillars: Healthier Planet, Healthier Products, and Healthier People. Following a 2022 shareholder proposal from Green Century and Clean Yield Asset Management, Hain committed to setting science-based greenhouse gas reduction targets covering its entire global footprint.

The company committed to the SBTi by end of 2023, establishing a FY2022 baseline with targets to reduce Scope 1&2 emissions by 42% and Scope 3 emissions by 25% by FY2030. Scope 3 represents approximately 91% of total emissions, making supply chain engagement the critical lever for meaningful decarbonization.

The transition plan is organized around four levers: renewable energy procurement, operational efficiency (including the landmark Histon biogas facility), sustainable packaging, and supply chain decarbonization through ingredient sourcing and logistics optimization.

Analyst Assessment

Hain Celestial's transition plan is credible in ambition — the 42% Scope 1&2 and 25% Scope 3 targets are consistent with a well-below-2°C pathway. However, the plan lacks detailed implementation milestones and quantified CapEx allocation. The 91% Scope 3 share means that achieving the 25% reduction target requires deep supply chain transformation, which Hain has limited leverage over as a mid-cap company ($1.74B revenue). The Sustainalytics ESG Risk Rating of 31.3 (High Risk) reflects both the ambition gap and execution uncertainty.

Sources: Hain Celestial 2023 Impact Report · Hain Celestial Climate Action Page · Green Century shareholder proposal disclosure (2022) · SBTi Target Dashboard

Hain Celestial's flagship decarbonization project is the anaerobic digestion plant at Histon, Cambridgeshire (UK). The facility converts fruit waste from production into biogas, which powers a combined heat and power (CHP) unit generating 25% of the site's electricity and providing hot water for manufacturing processes. A second CHP system was recently installed to further increase on-site renewable generation.

The Histon plant serves as a model for the company's global decarbonization strategy — demonstrating how waste-to-energy technology can simultaneously reduce emissions, cut energy costs, and divert organic waste from landfills. The facility is ISO 14001 certified.

Beyond the UK, Hain's 100% renewable energy target by 2025 requires scaling procurement across its global manufacturing network, though specific progress metrics by region have not been publicly detailed.

Gap Assessment

Hain Celestial's climate risk disclosures are among the weakest in the peer group. No formal scenario analysis has been published, and financial impacts of physical/transition risks are not quantified. The Sustainalytics ESG Risk Rating of 31.3 (High Risk) reflects both disclosure gaps and unmanaged risk exposure. ESRS E1-2 and E1-3 would require substantial improvement in scenario analysis methodology and quantified resilience assessments.

CSRD Readiness Gap

Hain Celestial's financial climate disclosures are the weakest area relative to ESRS requirements. ESRS E1-9 and E1-11 require quantified financial effects reconciled to financial statements. The company's ongoing restructuring and net losses further complicate climate investment planning. As a public US company with European operations, Hain may fall within CSRD scope and would need to substantially enhance these disclosures.
