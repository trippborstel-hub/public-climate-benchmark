ESRS E1-1 · CDP C3
Transition Plan for Climate Change Mitigation
Comprehensive

Nestlé published a comprehensive Net Zero Roadmap committing to eliminate GHG emissions across its full value chain by 2050. The plan covers all Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions. The 2018 baseline was ~91.2 Mt CO₂e. The plan was developed with SBTi validation and is aligned with a 1.5°C pathway. In 2023, Nestlé hit its 2025 interim target (20% reduction) two years early.

Analyst Assessment

Nestlé has one of the most ambitious transition plans in the food sector, with SBTi-validated 1.5°C targets. The 2025 interim target was achieved early (2023). The critical challenge is the 50.4% absolute reduction by 2030 — requiring massive acceleration in Scope 3 supply chain transformation, particularly in dairy and coffee sourcing. The no-offset stance (94% real reductions) is a strong differentiator.

Climate Risk Identification & Resilience
Partial

Nestlé faces significant climate risk exposure due to its heavily agricultural supply chain, spanning dairy, coffee, cocoa, cereals, and water-dependent operations across 185+ markets. The company has conducted scenario analysis and identifies both physical and transition risks, though quantified financial impact estimates remain limited in public disclosures.

ESRS E1-7 · CDP C11
Carbon Credits, Offsets & Removals
Comprehensive

Nestlé explicitly does NOT rely on offsets to meet its climate targets. 94% of the company's emissions decline came from actual reductions, not offsetting. Instead, Nestlé focuses on nature-based removals through regenerative agriculture programs and a commitment to plant 200 million trees by 2030, with 14.7 million secured in 2023.

CSRD Readiness Gap

ESRS E1-9 and E1-11 require quantified financial effects from physical and transition risks, reconciled to financial statements. Nestlé currently provides directional investment figures (CHF 3.2B climate commitment, CHF 1.2B regen agriculture) but not the granular risk quantification or asset-level exposure analysis that CSRD demands. As a Swiss-headquartered company with significant EU operations, Nestlé will need to close this gap for CSRD compliance.

08
ESRS 2 GOV · CDP C1
Governance & Accountability
Comprehensive

Nestlé has embedded climate governance into its corporate structure through a dedicated ESG and Sustainability Committee at the Board level. Executive compensation is directly tied to sustainability performance, with approximately 20% of long-term incentives linked to ESG metrics including climate targets. Sustainability leadership is embedded at the Executive Board level, ensuring climate strategy is integrated into core business decision-making.
