ESRS E1-1 · CDP C3
Transition Plan for Climate Change Mitigation
Comprehensive

Mars published a comprehensive Net Zero Roadmap in 2023, committing to eliminate greenhouse gas emissions across its full value chain by 2050. The plan covers all Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions — including agriculture (which represents ~60% of the footprint), manufacturing, logistics, and consumer use. The roadmap was developed in collaboration with SBTi and is aligned with a 1.5°C pathway under the Paris Agreement.

The plan is structured around five-year milestone targets with executive compensation directly tied to delivery. In 2024, Mars expanded the number of leaders with long-term compensation linked to GHG reductions from 400 to approximately 2,000.

Analyst Assessment

Mars has one of the most credible transition plans in the consumer goods sector. New Climate Institute analysis (April 2024) confirmed Mars has identified reduction measures capable of achieving >50% reduction by 2030. The key risk is execution speed — by 2021, only 6% reduction had been achieved, meaning 84% of the required progress must come in 2021–2030. Recent acceleration (8% in 2023 alone) is encouraging.

Mars' energy strategy combines renewable electricity procurement (PPAs and direct purchasing), on-site generation, energy efficiency improvements at manufacturing facilities, and emerging heat decarbonization through electrification. The company has pioneered its first electric heat project at a chocolate factory in the Netherlands, with plans to expand industrial heat decarbonization across the manufacturing network.

Energy efficiency improvements at manufacturing sites and technology-driven logistics optimization have been key drivers of Scope 1 and 2 reductions. Mars has achieved a 34% reduction in operational emissions since 2015 and is on track for its 63% reduction target by 2030.

Climate Risk Identification & Resilience
Partial

Mars has conducted climate scenario analysis aligned with TCFD recommendations, examining both physical and transition risks across its value chain. As a company heavily dependent on agricultural commodities (cocoa, dairy, rice, soy, palm oil), Mars faces significant exposure to both acute weather events and chronic shifts in growing conditions.

Gap Assessment

Mars' risk disclosures are strong on physical risk identification in agricultural supply chains but lack quantified financial impact estimates for most risks. Scenario analysis details (which scenarios, temperature pathways, time horizons) are not fully disclosed in public reports. Under ESRS E1-2 and E1-3, Mars would need to provide more granular scenario assumptions and quantified resilience assessments.

ESRS E1-7 · CDP C11
Carbon Credits, Offsets & Removals
Partial

Mars takes a "reductions first" approach consistent with ESRS requirements — all GHG targets are gross absolute targets with no credit netting. In 2024, Mars began accounting for land-based carbon removals (~42,000 tCO₂e) generated through climate-smart agriculture projects within its own supply chain, with 50% placed in a buffer pool to guard against reversal risk.

Notably, Mars discontinued carbon-neutral product claims in 2024 (Mars Bars in UK/Ireland/Canada and Royal Canin kitten/puppy products) after consumer demand failed to materialize, calling the program "not self-funding."

CSRD Readiness Gap

ESRS E1-9 and E1-11 require quantified financial effects from physical and transition risks, reconciled to financial statements. Mars currently provides directional investment figures but not the granular risk quantification or asset-level exposure analysis that CSRD demands. This is the most significant disclosure gap relative to ESRS requirements — though it is common across the sector.

ESRS 2 GOV · CDP C1
Governance & Accountability
Comprehensive

Mars has embedded climate governance deeply into its organizational structure. In 2024, the company created a dedicated Chief Climate Officer role (Kevin Rabinovitch, 30-year Mars veteran), alongside the Chief Sustainability Officer (Alastair Child). Climate targets are integrated into the company's "Compass" management system, which weights sustainability metrics alongside traditional business performance.
