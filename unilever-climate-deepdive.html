ESRS E1-1 · CDP C3
Transition Plan for Climate Change Mitigation
Comprehensive

Unilever published a comprehensive Climate Transition Action Plan (CTAP), updated in 2024, which was approved by 97.5% of shareholders — an exceptional level of governance alignment. The plan commits to eliminating greenhouse gas emissions across Scope 1, 2, and 3 by 2039, the most aggressive net zero target in the CPG sector. The CTAP covers operational decarbonization, supply chain transformation, and product reformulation across 190+ markets.

Unilever targets zero Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2030 and a 42% reduction in Scope 3 energy-related emissions by 2030 (vs. 2021 baseline). CapEx for climate is embedded directly within each Business Group's financial plans rather than treated as a separate sustainability budget.

Analyst Assessment

Unilever's 2039 net zero target is the most aggressive in the CPG sector. Already at 100% renewable electricity since 2020 (RE100 founding member), the focus is now on thermal energy decarbonization and massive Scope 3 supply chain transformation. The 97.5% shareholder approval of CTAP demonstrates exceptional governance alignment — one of the highest advisory vote results for any climate transition plan globally.

Unilever achieved 100% renewable electricity across ALL operations in January 2020 — factories, offices, R&D facilities, warehouses, and data centers globally. This was achieved at no net additional cost through a combination of Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs), on-site solar installations across 23 countries, and Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs).

With the electricity milestone achieved, the focus has shifted to 100% renewable thermal energy by 2030. This represents the next major frontier, involving heat pumps, biomass, waste heat recovery, and electrification of industrial processes. Cumulative energy cost savings have exceeded EUR 1 billion, demonstrating that the transition has been financially accretive.

ESRS E1-7 · CDP C11
Carbon Credits, Offsets & Removals
Partial

Unilever explicitly prioritizes absolute reductions over offsetting — this is a central principle of the Climate Transition Action Plan. The company does not rely on carbon credits to meet its near-term or net zero targets, instead focusing entirely on value chain decarbonization through supplier engagement, product reformulation, and operational improvements.

No internal carbon price has been disclosed, and no major carbon removal purchases have been publicly reported. The strategy is to drive real-world emissions reductions across the supply chain rather than compensate through market mechanisms.

Disclosure Gap

While Unilever embeds climate investments within BG financial plans (a strong operational practice), specific climate CapEx and OpEx are not publicly disaggregated. Under ESRS E1-9 and E1-11, more granular financial effect quantification — including asset-level exposure and transition cost breakdowns — would strengthen disclosure. The >EUR 1B energy savings figure is a notable positive signal that the transition is financially accretive.

ESRS 2 GOV · CDP C1
Governance & Accountability
Comprehensive

Unilever has embedded climate governance across multiple layers of board and executive oversight. Three board committees — the Corporate Responsibility Committee, Audit Committee, and Remuneration Committee — share oversight of climate strategy, risk, and incentive alignment. CEO Fernando Fernandez holds ultimate responsibility for climate and sustainability performance, with the Sustainability Progress Index incorporated into the Performance Share Plan (long-term incentive).
